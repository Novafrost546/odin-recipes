Desciption

Phở is Vietnam’s most iconic dish — a fragrant, comforting noodle soup that balances a rich, clear beef broth with delicate rice noodles and fresh herbs. Traditionally eaten for breakfast but loved any time of day, phở brings together layers of flavor: the sweetness of slow-simmered bones, the warmth of spices like star anise and cinnamon, and the freshness of basil, lime, and bean sprouts. The result is a perfect harmony of savory, aromatic, and refreshing.

Ingredients

For Broth

  • 3 lbs beef bones (marrow or knuckle bones)
  • 1 lb beef brisket (optional for serving)
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, halved lengthwise
  • 3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (plus more to taste)
  • 1 tbsp rock sugar or 1 tsp regular sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 4 quarts (16 cups) water

For the Noodles and Toppings

  • 1 lb dried or fresh rice noodles (bánh phở)
  • ½ lb thinly sliced raw beef sirloin or eye of round (for topping)
  • 1 small white onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Optional Garnishes (served on the side)

  • Fresh Thai basil
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Fresh chili slices or jalapeños
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Sriracha

Steps

  1. Place the onion and ginger directly over an open flame (or under the oven broiler) until the skin is slightly charred, about 5 minutes.
  2. Rinse off any burnt bits under warm water.
  3. Return the cleaned bones to the pot. Add brisket (if using), charred onion, and ginger.
  4. Add 4 quarts of fresh water.
  5. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Skim off any foam that rises.
  6. In a small pan, lightly toast the star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves, and coriander seeds for 2–3 minutes until fragrant. Add to the broth.
  7. Stir in fish sauce, rock sugar, and salt.
  8. Simmer uncovered for 3–4 hours, occasionally adding water if needed.
  9. Remove the bones, onion, and spices from the broth.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with more fish sauce or salt as desired.
  11. If you cooked brisket, slice it thinly and set aside for serving.
  12. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions.
  13. Drain and rinse briefly with cold water to prevent sticking.
  14. Divide noodles into bowls.
  15. Top with slices of brisket and raw beef.
  16. Ladle the hot broth over the beef — the heat will gently cook the thin slices.
  17. Garnish with onions, green onions, and cilantro.
  18. Serve with a platter of basil, lime, bean sprouts, chili, hoisin, and sriracha on the side.
